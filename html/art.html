"Simplicity is the keynote of all true elegance." - Coco Chanel

Art and Culture has been influenced by diverse artists. Some famous personalities have been involved in architectural designed, graphic designed, fashion design and industrial designed. In my opinion, they all have something in common, their search of simplicity and clarity through a subtile observation of their environment. Playing with shapes, structures, colors and textures they have created some wonderful pieces of art.

At school, during drawing lesson we would try to reproduce some techniques and drawing of Swiss designers, sculptors or drawers. I used to like drawing lines and dots or to sand and saw wood to build some useful containers.

Le Corbusier

Not only he was a well-known architect but also an excellent furniture designer. Here is one of his famous chair that can be found in many offices! I assure you, there are really confortable to take a little nap!

Alberto Giacometti

Famous sculpteur, he was on our 100 blue note. His extremely tall and slender figures havae something special! They ask for interrogation, they attract and appeal the eye and we need to stop to observe them!

Jean Tnguely

Movement! Country of lakes and rivers, we like to play with water! Tinguely's scluptures are found in many cities in Switzerland. When I was living in Basel, I would sit by the Tinguely's fountain and observed the repetitive movement of the metalic pieces. These sculptures are dacing like trees in the wind!

Paul Klee

A Museum is dedicated to him in Bern. I like his use of color and style of abstraction! His work hides some mysteries and trying to unlock them can be challenging!

Caran d'Ache

From russian carandache' meaning pencil, Caran d'Ache is a Swiss manufacturer of fixpencils, watercolour pencils and neocolors. We would always had some pencils boxes at home and school and I loved cut all my pencils sharpely to be able to draw thin and clear lines.

Helvetica

Typeface developed by Swiss designer Max Miedeinger, it has been a widely used by comercial wordmarks and government. You might have come across in the New York Tube or on your iPhone until 2015.
